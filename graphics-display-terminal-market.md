<p><p>The Graphics Display Terminal market is primarily divided into two types: raster and vector displays. Raster displays render images through a grid of pixels, making them ideal for detailed images and complex graphics but limited in scalability. In contrast, vector displays utilize mathematical equations to represent images, providing crisp lines and scalability without loss of quality, which is favorable for technical applications. Both types cater to distinct needs in graphic representation, influencing preferences across industries like design, engineering, and entertainment.</p></p>
