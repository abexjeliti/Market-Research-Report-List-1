<p><p>The digital retinal cameras market comprises several types: Hybrid cameras combine practices for versatile use, enabling comprehensive retinal imaging. Mydriatic cameras require pupil dilation to capture detailed images of the retina, ideal for comprehensive exams. Non-mydriatic cameras don't necessitate dilation, offering quick, patient-friendly imaging suitable for screening and monitoring. Retinopathy of Prematurity (ROP) cameras are specialized for neonatal use, designed to detect and monitor retinal conditions in premature infants, ensuring timely intervention and care.</p></p>
