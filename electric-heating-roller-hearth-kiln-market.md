Continuous kilns operate in a steady flow, allowing for uninterrupted production, making them ideal for large-scale manufacturing. Conversely, batch kilns process a set quantity of materials at a time, offering flexibility for varied product types. Both types are essential in industries such as ceramics and metal processing, with continuous kilns focusing on efficiency and mass production, while batch kilns provide versatility for smaller operations.</p></p>
