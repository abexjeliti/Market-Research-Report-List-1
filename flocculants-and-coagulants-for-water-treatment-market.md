<p><p>Flocculants and coagulants are essential chemicals used in water treatment to facilitate the removal of suspended particles. Inorganic types, such as aluminum sulfate and ferric chloride, work by destabilizing particles to promote aggregation. Organic types, including polyacrylamides and natural polyelectrolytes, enhance floc formation through charge neutralization and bridging mechanisms. Both types are critical for improving water clarity, reducing turbidity, and ensuring compliance with health and environmental regulations, catering to various industrial and municipal applications in the water treatment market.</p></p>
